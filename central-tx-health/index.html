About this data: Local safety-net health coverage programs in the six-county region. These programs serve low-income uninsured and underinsured residents not eligible for Medicaid/CHIP or ACA. Enrollment data is limited for non-Travis counties.
Local Safety-Net Programs Overview

Central Health Medical Access Program (MAP)

Operated by Central Health, the hospital district for Travis County. Provides primary and preventive care coverage to low-income uninsured residents of Travis County with income up to 200% of federal poverty level (FPL). Enrollment: approximately 126,000 (FY2024).

County Indigent Health Care Programs (CIHCP)

Required by state law in all 6 counties, these programs provide basic emergency and essential care to county residents with income at or below 21% of FPL (approximately $300/month for an individual). Each county operates its CIHCP separately; enrollment varies by county. Non-Travis counties typically serve 5,000–20,000 individuals, though exact figures are not centrally reported.

Coverage Gaps

Many low-income uninsured residents—including undocumented immigrants, mixed-status families, and those with incomes above CIHCP thresholds but below Medicaid limits—remain uninsured. These local programs fill some, but not all, gaps left by public programs.

Data Sources: HHSC Healthcare Statistics (Medicaid/CHIP enrollment) • Census SAHIE (model-based uninsured estimates, 2019–2023) • ACS 1-Year Estimates (Tables S2701, B27001; 2019, 2021–2024) • CMS Marketplace PUFs (ACA Open Enrollment plan selections, 2020–2025) • Central Health (Medical Access Program enrollment)
