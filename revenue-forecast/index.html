Forecasting Methodology (v4 Refined Model)

The v4 model uses a ten-step process combining seasonal decomposition, trend estimation, actual-data calibration, policy-informed adjustments, live economic assumption updates, dynamic CRE weighting, bottom-up annual construction, and probabilistic range estimation with temporal uncertainty scaling.

Key changes from v2/v3: The model now (1) builds the annual total bottom-up from actuals + projected remaining, (2) derives CRE blend weights empirically from actual-to-CRE deviation, (3) adjusts CRE assumptions using live economic data from the EIA and Federal Reserve, and (4) scales probability cone widths by √t from last known data point.

Step 1: Seasonal Index Estimation

For each category, we compute the average share of annual total that each month represents over the five most recent complete fiscal years (FY 2021–2025). This yields 12 seasonal indices per category summing to 1.0.

COVID Exclusion: For Federal Income, FY 2020 and FY 2021 are excluded from index computation because COVID-era relief programs (CRF, enhanced FMAP, ARPA) distorted the first-half share from a normal 51–53% down to 36–39%.

Step 2: Annual Level Trend

A weighted linear regression is fitted to the five most recent complete fiscal year annual totals, with more recent years receiving proportionally higher weight.

Step 3: Growth Momentum

The three-year average year-over-year growth rate captures near-term acceleration or deceleration in each category’s trajectory.

Step 4: Blended Annual Projection

Trend-based and growth-rate-based projections are blended 60/40, then calibrated against actual FY 2026 collections using a 70/30 weighting (actual-implied / model). This produces the model’s independent annual estimate (model_annual).

Step 5: Outlier Detection & Exclusion

Before calibration, the model applies a z-score > 3.0 threshold to identify anomalous FY 2026 months. Outlier months are excluded from the implied-annual calculation but are still displayed as actuals.

Example: Other Revenue in October 2025 recorded $2.06B vs. a historical mean of ~$144M (z = 8.9). Excluding this outlier moved the forecast from +170% above CRE to +10% above CRE.

Step 6: Economic Assumption Update (NEW in v4)

For categories with direct price or rate linkages, the CRE’s annual estimate is adjusted using current market data before it enters the blending process. This corrects for stale assumptions in the original CRE without replacing its structural model.

Step 8: Dynamic CRE Weighting via MAPE

Rather than using fixed CRE blend weights, the model measures how well the (adjusted) CRE tracks reality and adjusts its influence accordingly:

Sub-stepDescription
8a. CRE Implied MonthlyAdjusted CRE annual × seasonal indices → 12 CRE-implied monthly values
8b. Measure MAPEMean Absolute Percentage Error of actuals vs. CRE-implied for months with data
8c. Compute Dynamic Weightdynamic_weight = prior_weight × exp(−5 × MAPE)

Interpretation: Low MAPE → CRE retains strong influence. High MAPE → model’s independent projection dominates. The economic assumption update in Step 6 improves the adjusted CRE’s alignment with actuals, which in turn increases its dynamic weight for categories with stale price assumptions.

Step 9: Bottom-Up Annual Construction

This is the core architectural change from v2. Instead of distributing a top-down annual across all months:

ComponentDescription
Actual YTDSum of actual collections for months with real data (Sep 2025–Feb 2026)
Model Remainingmodel_annual − actual_ytd (model’s view of what remains)
CRE Remainingadjusted_cre_annual − actual_ytd (adjusted CRE’s view)
Blended Remainingdynamic_weight × cre_remaining + (1 − dynamic_weight) × model_remaining
Monthly ProjectionBlended remaining distributed across future months by renormalized seasonal shares
Annual TotalActual YTD + Sum(Projected Months) — the annual is an output, not an input

Why this matters: Known actual collections are preserved exactly. Only future months are projected. The model never overwrites reality with a seasonal approximation.

Step 10: Confidence Tiers & √t Probability Cones

Each category is assigned a confidence tier based on seasonal stability and growth volatility. Cones incorporate two important refinements:

RefinementDescription
Actuals = Zero UncertaintyMonths with actual data show P10 = P25 = P50 = P75 = P90 = actual value (no cone)
√t Temporal WideningCone widths grow with √(months ahead): March = √1, August = √6 (widest)

Limitations

Policy overlays are manual — adjustment factors are based on research available as of March 2026 and represent informed judgment, not statistical estimation.

Dynamic CRE weighting sensitivity — the decay parameter (α = 5) in the exponential weighting formula was chosen for reasonable behavior but has not been backtested against historical fiscal years.

Elasticity estimates are judgment-based — the price-to-revenue elasticities (0.60–0.85) for the economic assumption update have not been econometrically estimated from historical Texas revenue data.

Calendar-to-fiscal year conversion is approximate — EIA STEO forecasts are calendar-year; FY weighting (33/67) is a simplification.

Probability cones assume symmetric distributions — actual revenue distributions may be skewed. The √t widening approximates but does not model fat tails.

Limited economic indicator coverage — currently only oil, gas, and interest rates are updated live. Employment, GSP, and CPI could be added in future versions.

Legislative risk — 89th Legislature session outcomes may alter revenue trajectories.
